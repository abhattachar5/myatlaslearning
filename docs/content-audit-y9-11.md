# Atlas — Y9–11 Content Granularity Audit (read-only, no edits)

**Date:** 2026-06 · **Scope:** Year 9–11, all five subjects · **Reference spec:** AQA GCSE
**Method:** Counted islands per topic, compared against the AQA specification, flagged topics that are
under-subdivided and listed the specific missing islands. No content files were modified.

---

## Why this exists

Year 7 topics average **~6 islands** each (hand-crafted, richly subdivided — e.g. Vocabulary = 9,
Grammar = 10). Year 9–11 were mass-produced later on a flat template (**~3 islands** for English,
**~2–3** for the others). The dead-flat ranges (3–3, 2–3) are the fingerprint of templating rather than
curriculum decomposition. The result is **inverted from what you'd want**: KS3 is richly subdivided
while GCSE — the highest-stakes years — is the thinnest.

| Y8 English | 4.1 | 4–5 |

## The key distinction: content gaps vs granularity gaps

Not all "under-subdivided" findings are equal. Two kinds emerged:

1. **TRUE CONTENT GAPS — whole AQA sub-sections that have *no island anywhere*.** These are the
   highest value: a student literally cannot revise the topic in the app. Examples:
   - Science: **Reversible reactions & equilibrium** (entire spec section, absent); **acid–base salt
     preparations & redox**; **Hooke's law / elasticity** (required practical); **I–V characteristics**
     (required practical); **selective breeding & genetic engineering**.
   - Geography: the **Food/Water/Energy resource-management option**; the **two contrasting fieldwork
     enquiries + GIS/statistics** (Paper 3); the **mandatory LIC/NEE city case study**.
   - History: the **historic-environment site studies** (Elizabethan, Norman, Western Front sources) and
     the **synoptic "factors in progress" islands** (Medicine, Power & the People, Health) — both are
     effectively mandated exam components currently missing.
   - Maths: **3D Pythagoras** (topic scoped "2D only"); **factorising ax²+bx+c (a≠1)**; **spheres/cones/
     pyramids**; **3D trig**; **alternate segment theorem**.
   - English: the **Language Paper 1 & 2 reading questions** are bundled — Q1/Q2/Q3 are distinct AQA
     questions (Q3 Structure is the lowest-scoring nationally) crammed into one island.

2. **GRANULARITY GAPS — content present but compressed**, where one island should be split into two or
   three (e.g. Geography "River Landforms" → erosional + depositional; English "Prose & Drama" → methods
   + context + whole-text tracking). Valuable for usability, lower urgency than (1).

**Maths is the least affected** — its question generators give every island deep practice, so its gaps
are genuine missing *concepts*, not thin coverage. It needs the fewest additions.

## Recommended roadmap (priority tiers)

### Tier 1 — True content gaps (build first; highest exam value)
- **Science Y10 chemistry/physics:** reversible reactions & equilibrium; acids/salts + redox; Hooke's
  law + work/energy/power; I–V characteristics. **Science Y11:** selective breeding & genetic engineering.
  **Science Y10 biology:** split blood-glucose/diabetes and contraception/fertility.
- **Geography Y10:** Resource Management (Food/Water/Energy option); Skills & Fieldwork (2 enquiries +
  GIS + statistics); LIC/NEE city case study.
- **History (whichever options the school sits):** historic-environment site-study islands; "factors in
  progress" synoptic islands; Medicine 19th-c/public-health/surgery split; Power & the People
  Reform/Chartism/unions/modern-rights; Germany police-state / daily-life / persecution split.
- **English:** split Language Paper 1 (Q2 Language, Q3 Structure) and Paper 2 (Q2 Summary/Synthesis, Q3
  Single-source language); add 19th-c non-fiction reading.
- **Maths:** 3D Pythagoras; factorising ax²+bx+c; spheres/cones/pyramids; 3D trig; alternate segment.

### Tier 2 — Granularity splits (content exists, compressed)
- English Prose & Drama (methods/context/whole-text); Y11 two-island topics (planning/retrieval/criteria).
- Geography rivers & coasts (erosional vs depositional), Natural Hazards, Living World.
- History period studies (Conflict & Tension peacemaking/escalation; American West; Germany).
- Maths medium-priority sub-skills (graphical inequalities, exponential graphs, algebraic-fraction
  equations, etc.).

### Tier 3 — Y9 bridging (lowest priority; thin is defensible for a foundation year)
- Y9 splits across all subjects (ecosystems, rivers, coasts, urbanisation, decolonisation, etc.).

### Structural fix to review (not an island count)
- **History h11t-03 "Britain: Health & the People" duplicates h10t-01 "Medicine Through Time"** (same
  ~750-year AQA thematic study, half the islands). Decide whether these should be merged/de-duplicated
  rather than both expanded.

## Per-subject detail

> Verdicts use: **Adequate** | **Under-subdivided**. Priorities: High / Medium / Low.
> History/Geography note: AQA candidates sit only a *subset* of options, so recommendations are kept at
> the level of named spec sub-themes — every topic a school actually teaches should carry its listed islands.

### MATHS — 11/42 under-subdivided · ~18 new islands
Top gaps: 3D Pythagoras (m10t-07, "2D only"); factorising ax²+bx+c a≠1 (m10t-03); alternate segment
theorem (m11t-07); spheres/cones/pyramids (m9t-12); 3D trig (m10t-08).
Other under-subdivided: m9t-09 (rearranging formulae, algebraic indices); m9t-12 (arcs/sectors);
m10t-01 (graphical/quadratic inequalities); m10t-11 (compound-unit conversion); m11t-02 (exponential
graphs); m11t-04 (solving algebraic-fraction equations); m11t-10 (solving trig equations graphically).
Generators mean maths needs the fewest additions of any subject.

### ENGLISH — 11/23 under-subdivided · ~23 new islands
Top gaps (High): e10t-05 Analysing Non-fiction (whole of Lang Paper 2 reading in 3 islands — add
summary/synthesis, 19th-c non-fiction, single-source language); e11t-06 Lang Paper 2 (split Q2/Q3);
e11t-05 Lang Paper 1 (split Q2 Language, Q3 Structure — national weak spot).
Medium: e10t-08 Prose & Drama (methods AO2, context AO3, whole-text tracking); e10t-06 Writing
(speeches, FAP); e9t-05 Reading (inference/retrieval, building a paragraph); e9t-08 Shakespeare
(context, essay-writing). Systemic: four Y11 topics (e11t-01/02/07/09) sit at 2 islands — one below
template — each missing a planning/retrieval/criteria island the revision year needs.
Set-text topics (Macbeth, A Christmas Carol, An Inspector Calls, Power & Conflict) all confirmed adequate (6 islands each).

### SCIENCE — 16/42 under-subdivided · ~33 new islands
**These include true content gaps, not just granularity.** Top gaps (High): s10t-09 Rates (add
**reversible reactions & equilibrium** — entire section absent); s10t-07 Chemical Changes (acids/bases/
salt-making + redox/half-equations); s11t-02 Evolution (selective breeding & genetic engineering —
whole sub-section absent); s10t-14 Forces (Hooke's law/elasticity required practical + work/energy/
power); s10t-05 Hormonal (split blood-glucose/diabetes + contraception/fertility).
Other under-subdivided: s9t-09 (strong/weak acids, making salts), s9t-13 (reflection/refraction
practical, sound), s10t-02 (CHD/treatments, risk factors), s10t-03 (plant diseases, drug trials),
s10t-04 (kidneys/water balance), s10t-11 (I–V characteristics practical, static), s10t-12 (latent heat,
gas pressure), s11t-03 (biodiversity/human impact), s11t-04 (LCA, corrosion/alloys, water treatment),
s11t-09 (v–t graphs, terminal velocity). Y9 chemistry foundation (atomic structure, bonding) already
well-subdivided — the model split.

### HISTORY — 9/23 under-subdivided · ~30 new islands
Top gaps (High): h10t-01 Medicine Through Time (~750-yr thematic study in 3 islands — split 19th-c
revolution, surgery, public health, + "factors" thread); h10t-07 Power & the People (~850-yr study;
Reform/Chartism, abolition, unions, modern rights missing); h11t-03 Health & the People (2 islands,
overlaps Medicine — de-duplicate); h11t-01 Germany (split Nazi police-state / daily-life / persecution);
h10t-02 Western Front (add the source / historic-environment island — a mandated component).
Medium: h10t-03 Elizabethan (Poor Laws, Armada, site study); h10t-08 Norman (resistance, site study);
h10t-04 Conflict & Tension (peacemaking, escalation); h11t-04 American West (Mormons, homesteading,
Plains Indians). **Two systematic omissions: historic-environment site studies and synoptic "factors"
islands — highest-value, most curriculum-grounded additions.** Skills/exam-technique topics all adequate.

### GEOGRAPHY — 14/23 under-subdivided · ~41 new islands
Top gaps (all Y10, the primary teaching year): g10t-07 Resource Management (2→6: overview + full
Food/Water/Energy option); g10t-08 Skills & Fieldwork (2→7: GIS, statistics, two contrasting fieldwork
enquiries — underpins Paper 3); g10t-05 Urban Issues (missing the mandatory LIC/NEE city case study);
g10t-01 Natural Hazards (3→7: tropical storms, management, UK weather case study); g10t-02 Living World
(hot-desert opportunities, deforestation case study).
Medium: rivers (g10t-03) & coasts (g10t-04) erosional vs depositional splits; Y11 g11t-02 (ecosystems),
g11t-04 (split energy vs water), g11t-05 (two fieldwork enquiries). Y9 splits (ecosystems, rivers,
coasts, urbanisation) lower priority as bridging.

## Suggested next step
Pick a **Tier 1 slice** to build first as a pilot (recommended: **Science Y10 chemistry** or
**Geography Y10**, where the true content gaps are most damaging), establish the per-island quality bar,
then roll out subject-by-subject. Each new island = 5 flashcards + 5 MCQs + 1 lesson (+ diagram where
visual), wired into topics.js + manifest, verified, committed.
